# Learning Outcome and Assessment Alignment

This matrix shows where each learning outcome is introduced or practiced and where students provide assessable evidence. Weeks indicate the principal alignment, not every occasion on which a skill appears.

| Outcome | Main learning activities | Principal assessment evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Formulate a focused global question | Weeks 1, 4, 6, and capstone workshops | Mini-projects and capstone question framing |
| 2. Locate and evaluate data | Weeks 3, 6, and 11 | Mini-project 1 data documentation; capstone data dictionary |
| 3. Import, clean, transform, merge, and summarize data | Weeks 2-4 and labs 01-02 | Mini-project 1 and Week 8 midterm |
| 4. Create accurate and ethical visualizations | Week 5 and visualization critique | Mini-project 2 and capstone visual report |
| 5. Apply statistical reasoning and introductory prediction | Weeks 7, 9, and 10 | Week 8 midterm; model-evaluation activity; capstone where appropriate |
| 6. Distinguish description, association, prediction, and causation | Weeks 1, 5, 7, and 9 | Midterm interpretation; Mini-project 2; capstone limitations |
| 7. Conduct transparent basic text analysis | Week 11 | AI Verification and Text-Data Exercise |
| 8. Use and verify LLM assistance responsibly | Weeks 12-13 and lab 03 | AI Verification and Text-Data Exercise; AI Use Statements |
| 9. Design a bounded workflow with human oversight | Week 13 and lab 04 | Bounded-Agent Prototype; capstone AI Workflow Card |
| 10. Produce and communicate reproducible analysis | Weeks 6, 14-16 | Both mini-projects and the final capstone package |

## Alignment review questions

Before releasing an assessment, the instructor should confirm that:

1. the task asks for observable evidence of the intended outcome;
2. the rubric evaluates that evidence directly;
3. students have already practiced the required workflow;
4. technical difficulty does not obscure the analytical objective; and
5. accessibility and permitted-tool conditions are stated.
