# Final Team Capstone Project Brief

## Goal

Answer a real global decision-making question through transparent, reproducible, and responsible analysis.

## Approved Tracks

- Trade and market entry
- International finance and macroeconomic risk
- Global consumer behavior and digital markets
- Logistics and supply chains
- Development, inequality, and sustainability
- International relations, policy, and public opinion
- Financial, corporate, or ESG data
- Text and communication analysis

## Required Deliverables

1. Well-organized GitHub repository.
2. Reproducible Python notebook or report.
3. Data dictionary.
4. Visual executive report or dashboard-style output.
5. Two-page decision memo.
6. AI Workflow Card.
7. Short final presentation.
8. Individual reflection or short defense.

## Milestone Schedule

- Week 10: teams and preliminary topic confirmed.
- Week 13: capstone progress check and AI Workflow Card draft.
- Week 14: capstone draft peer review.
- Week 15: final repository, notebook/report, data dictionary, decision memo, visual outputs, and AI Workflow Card due.
- Week 16: presentation and individual defense/reflection.

## Team Process

- At Week 10, record each member's initial role and a shared communication plan.
- At Weeks 13 and 14, update the contribution record and raise unresolved participation concerns with the instructor.
- All members should review the final repository, conclusions, limitations, and AI Workflow Card.
- The individual defense or reflection may adjust the separate individual component but does not replace timely team communication.

## Submission Standard

The repository must include run instructions, data provenance, dependency information, and a clear map of deliverables. Raw third-party data should only be redistributed when its license permits; otherwise document retrieval steps. Date-specific deadlines and presentation length will be confirmed in the LMS.
